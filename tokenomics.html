How VCLM, CHONX, and SYNTH are earned, governed, and made scarce.

The architecture

Three tokens. Each necessary for the next.

Not three tokens
that happen to coexist.
Three tokens that require each other.

VCLM is earned by locking supported assets. CHONX is earned by staking VCLM — at 1.5× the rate for VCLM holders, hard-coded and permanent. SYNTH can only be created by permanently burning both. Each token is necessary for the existence of the next. None of them can be purchased. All of them must be earned.

The three token supplies maintain a consistent proportional relationship at every level — 10 billion VCLM, 100 billion CHONX, 10 million SYNTH. The burn ratio mirrors this: 1,000 VCLM and 10,000 CHONX to mint 1 SYNTH. The proportions are not arbitrary. They are designed.

How VCLM is earned

Commitment, not purchase

The emission rate on
day one is the highest
it will ever be.

VCLM minted = (USD value of deposit) × asset multiplier × duration multiplier × emission factor. The emission factor launches at 10 VCLM per $1.00 and decays at 1.667% per month from the protocol deployment timestamp. This decay is automatic, irreversible, and cannot be paused or overridden by governance. The permanent floor is 1 VCLM per $1.00 — decay stops there forever.

The maximum lock duration is 36 months. Every lock expires with 95% of the committed asset returned to the participant. VCLM arrives in the wallet the instant the lock confirms — not at expiry. The discipline is to lock that returning asset again immediately, at a longer duration than last time.

01

Duration multipliers

7 days: 1.0× · 30 days: 1.0× · 90 days: 1.25× · 180 days: 1.5× · 365 days: 2.0× · 36 months: 3.0×. Longer commitment earns more VCLM per dollar. The maximum lock of 36 months earns three times the VCLM of a 30-day lock on the same deposit.

02

The restaking discipline

When a vault expires, 95% of your committed supported asset returns. Lock it again immediately — always at a longer duration than before. The people who do this consistently build positions that cannot be replicated by later participants at any price. The combination of decay and duration multipliers means early restakers are permanently advantaged.

03

Three activities can run simultaneously

After CHONX launches, three things can run at the same time: locking supported assets to earn VCLM, staking non-reserve VCLM for CHONX at 1.5×, and burning VCLM and CHONX to mint SYNTH. The vault does not close when CHONX or SYNTH launches. Locking continues as long as the VCLM hard cap allows.

The burn mechanic

What makes SYNTH permanent

What the burn does
Destroys VCLM and CHONX permanently
The burned tokens do not go to a treasury. They are not redistributed. They cease to exist. The total possible supply of both tokens decreases with every SYNTH minted.
Creates headroom below the hard cap
As burns destroy VCLM, the gap between total minted supply and the 10 billion hard cap reopens. New vault locks can mint into that headroom. The hard cap is a ceiling the protocol's own participants push downward through voluntary destruction.
Produces the burn-mint oscillation
Under high demand, burns create headroom, new minting fills it, newly minted VCLM gets burned again for more SYNTH. The cycle becomes self-sustaining. The protocol enters a state where creation and destruction run simultaneously.
Hard cap warning appears at 1%
When remaining mintable supply of VCLM or CHONX drops below 1% of the hard cap, the vault interface displays the remaining supply live and suggests a smaller lock. The contract will revert oversized locks — this is correct behavior. Try a smaller amount.
How to burn correctly
Set your SYNTH target first
Decide how many SYNTH you want before staking a single token for CHONX. Work backward: each SYNTH requires 1,000 VCLM and 10,000 CHONX. Your VCLM reserve is set from this calculation and does not change.
Never stake your reserve VCLM for CHONX
Your SYNTH reserve VCLM must never be staked. It is already spoken for. Only non-reserve VCLM goes to CHONX staking. This distinction matters — confusing reserve and non-reserve VCLM is the most consequential mistake in this protocol.
Calculate the exact ratio before burning
1,000 VCLM to 10,000 CHONX per SYNTH — precisely. Count your reserve VCLM. Count your CHONX. If the ratio is off in either direction, go to an exchange and buy exactly the shortfall. Not approximately. Every unpaired token is SYNTH you could have minted.
Burn early — ingredients become scarcer over time
As more participants burn, the compression cascade tightens the supply of both VCLM and CHONX. Future burns become progressively more expensive in ingredient terms. It is better to be ready early with slightly less SYNTH than to be late when ingredients are scarce.

What is fixed

And what governance decides

Some things are
written in the contract.
Nothing changes them.

Certain rules are immutable — locked in the smart contracts and unreachable by any governance vote, admin key, or upgrade. They are enforced by code alone. Violations are technically impossible, not merely prohibited.

Everything else is governed by token holders through on-chain proposals. Standard proposals require greater than 50% quorum, greater than 60% approval, and a 14-day public voting window. Proposal deposits are refunded on pass or quorum — forfeited if the proposal fails to reach quorum.

Immutable — no governance vote can ever change these

The emission rate can only decrease. The 1.667% monthly decay is a floor on direction — it will never reverse.
SYNTH burn formula
1,000 VCLM and 10,000 CHONX per SYNTH. Fixed in the contract. Cannot be changed.
No presale, no premine, no insider allocation
There is no minting path that bypasses commitment vaults for VCLM. Every token was earned.
Governable — decided by token holders through proposals
Supported asset list
Which tokens are approved for commitment vaults. New assets can be proposed, reviewed, and added by governance vote.
Treasury distributions
How much flows from the treasury into the governance reward pool, and how often. The 50/30/20 tier split is fixed — the timing and amount are not.
Treasury split
The current canonical split of the 5% vault fee is 60% Core Reserve and 40% Dev Fund. This is governable within the boundaries set by the founding documents.
Chain expansion
Which new blockchains Vinculum deploys to. Every new chain brings a new community. Governance decides when and where.
Protocol partnerships
Strategic decisions about ecosystem integrations, exchange listings, and protocol growth — decided by the community that built it.
Audit and security requirements
Prior to mainnet launch, all contracts must be audited by at least one independent third-party firm. The report shall be published publicly. Governance may require additional audits.
