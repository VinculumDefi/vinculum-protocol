The architecture

Three tokens. Each necessary for the next.

Not three tokens that happen to coexist.
Three tokens that require each other.

VCLM is earned by locking supported assets. CHONX is earned by staking VCLM — at 1.5× the rate for VCLM holders, hard-coded and permanent. SYNTH can only be created by permanently burning both. Each token is necessary for the existence of the next. None of them can be purchased. All of them must be earned.

The three token supplies maintain a consistent proportional relationship: 10 billion VCLM, 100 billion CHONX, 10 million SYNTH. The burn ratio mirrors this — 1,000 VCLM and 10,000 CHONX to mint 1 SYNTH. The proportions are not arbitrary. They are designed.

How VCLM is earned

Commitment, not purchase

VCLM minted = (USD value of deposit) x asset multiplier x duration multiplier x emission factor. The emission factor launches at 10 VCLM per $1.00 and decays at 1.667% per month permanently. The floor is 1 VCLM per $1.00. The maximum lock duration is 36 months. VCLM arrives in the wallet the instant the lock confirms — not at expiry. 95% of the committed asset returns when the vault matures.

Some participants may choose to relock their returning asset at a longer duration than their previous lock. Others may not. The emission decay formula means earlier participants earn more VCLM per dollar than later ones at any given duration — this is a mechanical fact of how the formula works, not a financial projection or promise of any outcome. There are different strategies, and what fits one participant may not fit another.

01

Duration multipliers

7 days: 1.0x · 30 days: 1.0x · 90 days: 1.25x · 180 days: 1.5x · 365 days: 2.0x · 36 months: 3.0x. Longer commitment earns more VCLM per dollar. The maximum lock of 36 months earns three times the VCLM of a 30-day lock on the same deposit.

02

The restaking discipline

When a vault expires, 95% of your committed supported asset returns to your wallet. Some participants may choose to relock that asset — at the same or a longer duration — while others may not. The duration multipliers mean longer locks produce more VCLM per dollar at any given emission rate. What you do with your returned asset is entirely your own decision, based on your own goals and circumstances. Locking continues as long as the VCLM hard cap allows, even after CHONX and SYNTH have launched.

03

Three activities simultaneously

After CHONX launches, three things can run at once: locking supported assets to earn VCLM, staking non-reserve VCLM for CHONX at 1.5x, and burning VCLM and CHONX to mint SYNTH. The vault does not close when CHONX or SYNTH launches. They are independent parallel processes.

The burn mechanic

What makes SYNTH permanent

What the burn does
Destroys VCLM and CHONX permanently
The burned tokens do not go to a treasury. They are not redistributed. They cease to exist. The total possible supply of both tokens decreases with every SYNTH minted.
Creates headroom below the hard cap
As burns destroy VCLM, the gap between total minted supply and the 10 billion hard cap reopens. New vault locks can mint into that headroom. The hard cap is a ceiling the protocol's own participants push downward through voluntary destruction.
Produces the burn-mint oscillation
Under high demand, burns create headroom, new minting fills it, newly minted VCLM gets burned again for more SYNTH. The cycle becomes self-sustaining — creation and destruction running simultaneously.
Hard cap warning appears at 1%
When remaining mintable supply drops below 1% of the hard cap, the vault interface displays the remaining supply live and suggests a smaller lock. The contract will revert oversized locks — this is correct behavior. Try a smaller amount.
How to burn correctly
Understanding your SYNTH reserve
Some participants may find it helpful to determine a SYNTH target before staking any VCLM for CHONX, so they can calculate how much VCLM to set aside. Each SYNTH requires 1,000 VCLM and 10,000 CHONX. How much SYNTH to pursue — if any — is a personal decision based on individual goals and circumstances.
Reserve VCLM and staking VCLM serve different purposes
VCLM intended for a SYNTH burn cannot simultaneously be staked for CHONX — once staked, it is committed to that purpose. Participants who plan to mint SYNTH may want to track which portion of their VCLM is intended for burning versus staking. This is a mechanical consideration, not advice on what to do.
The burn ratio is fixed at 1,000 VCLM to 10,000 CHONX per SYNTH
The contract requires precisely this ratio. If a participant's VCLM and CHONX holdings do not match this ratio at burn time, only the paired portion can be used. Unpaired tokens remain in the wallet. Some participants may choose to source any shortfall on an exchange before burning — others may adjust the amount of SYNTH they mint to match what they have. These are mechanical options, not instructions.
Supply dynamics over time
As more participants burn VCLM and CHONX for SYNTH, the circulating supply of both contracts. The compression cascade means future burns may require sourcing ingredients from a smaller available supply. When participants may choose to burn is a personal consideration — this is simply how the mechanics interact over time.

What is fixed

And what governance decides

Some things are written in the contract.
Nothing changes them.

Certain rules are immutable — locked in the smart contracts and unreachable by any governance vote, admin key, or upgrade. They are enforced by code alone. Violations are technically impossible, not merely prohibited by policy.

Everything else is governed by token holders through on-chain proposals. Standard proposals require greater than 50% quorum, greater than 60% approval, and a 14-day public voting window. Proposal deposits are refunded on pass or quorum — forfeited if the proposal fails to reach quorum.
