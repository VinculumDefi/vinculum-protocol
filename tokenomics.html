Immutable
Rule 1.2
VCLM Is Not Stakeable

VCLM tokens are permanently and technically excluded from the staking system in Protocol 1. A user may not stake VCLM in any form — locked, unlocked, vested, or unvested — to earn new VCLM rewards, mints, or any protocol incentive. This restriction is enforced at the smart contract level and cannot be overridden by policy alone. See Section 6 for conditions under which VCLM staking may become available in a future protocol.

Rule 1.3
No Derivative Staking

Wrapped versions of VCLM, synthetic VCLM, or any token whose value is primarily derived from VCLM shall also be ineligible for staking. The protocol's smart contracts shall enforce this at the contract level, not merely by policy.

Section 02
VCLM Mint Formula
Rule 2.1
Mint Trigger
A VCLM mint is triggered only when a user deposits an approved external collateral asset (Rule 1.1) into a qualifying commitment vault. No other action triggers a mint.
Rule 2.2
Mint Formula

The amount of VCLM minted is determined by the following formula:

VCLM Minted = (USD Value of Deposit) × Quality Multiplier × Commitment Duration Multiplier × Emission Factor

The protocol calculates the USD value of any deposit using the asset's spot price at the exact moment of deposit. Users are responsible for calculating their own expected VCLM yield using the previewMint() function before committing. No specific deposit amount in any asset is guaranteed to produce a fixed VCLM output — the output is always a function of the four variables in the formula.

Rule 2.3
Quality Multiplier

The quality multiplier is assigned by asset tier as defined in Rule 1.1:

Rule 2.5
Emission Factor and Decay

The emission factor defines how many VCLM are minted per $1.00 USD of adjusted deposit value.

  • At launch: 10 VCLM per $1.00 USD (equivalent to $0.10 per VCLM)
  • Decay rate: 20% per year, computed on-chain from the protocol deployment timestamp
  • Floor: 1 VCLM per $1.00 USD — decay stops at this level permanently
  • The decay is automatic, irreversible, and cannot be paused or overridden by governance

Early participants earn significantly more VCLM per dollar committed than later participants. This is by design.

Rule 2.6
Example Calculation

A user deposits $3,000 USD worth of SHIB (Tier C) into a 730-day vault in the protocol's first week:

$3,000 × 0.70 (Tier C) × 3.00 (730-day) × 10 (launch emission) = 63,000 VCLM

The same deposit made one year later would yield less due to emission decay.

Section 03
Trial Vault Rules
Rule 3.1
Trial Vault Entry
Any approved asset may be deposited into a Trial Vault with a 7-day commitment period. A 2.5% retention fee is applied at entry.
Rule 3.2
Trial Vault Exit
A user who exits the Trial Vault before the 7-day period ends receives 97.5% of their principal back and retains all VCLM earned. The 2.5% retention is permanent and non-refundable.
Rule 3.3
Trial Vault Continuation
A user who continues from the Trial Vault into a standard vault incurs an additional 2.5% retention fee at continuation, for a total of 5% — consistent with standard vault retention.
Section 04
Supply Integrity Controls
Immutable
Rule 4.1
Hard Maximum Supply
VCLM has a hard maximum supply cap of 10,000,000,000 (ten billion) tokens. This cap is encoded in the VinculumToken smart contract and is immutable. No governance proposal, founder action, or other mechanism may increase this cap under any circumstances. Once the cap is reached, no further minting is possible regardless of collateral deposited.
Rule 4.2
Epoch Mint Cap
No more than 10,000,000 VCLM (0.1% of maximum supply) may be minted in any 7-day epoch across all users combined. This is the recommended starting cap and may be adjusted by governance vote. If the epoch cap is reached before the epoch ends, new mint requests are queued and processed automatically at the start of the next epoch in the order they were received. See Section 4A for priority queue rules governing queue position advancement.
Rule 4.3
Collateral Backing Ratio

The protocol shall track a Collateral Backing Ratio (CBR) defined as:

CBR = Total External Collateral (USD value) ÷ Total VCLM in Circulation (USD value)

If the CBR falls below 0.50, all new mints are automatically paused until the ratio recovers above the threshold.

Rule 4.4
Per-Asset Deposit Cap
Governance may set a maximum total deposit cap per approved asset to prevent any single asset from dominating the collateral pool. A cap of 0 means unlimited. Caps are adjustable by governance vote.
Rule 4.5
Treasury Split

At the time of each vault deposit, the protocol's 5% retention is atomically and permanently split as follows:

  • 82% → Core Reserve Pool
  • 12% → Dev Fund (founder-controlled multisig)
  • 6% → Ecosystem Fund (governed by VCLM holders)
Section 4A
Priority Queue
Rule 4A.1
Queue Formation
When the epoch mint cap is reached before an epoch ends, all remaining mint requests enter a first-in-first-out queue. Standard queue positions are processed in the order received at the start of the next available epoch at no additional cost.
Rule 4A.2
Priority Queue Eligibility
Priority queue access is available only to wallets that have successfully completed at least one prior vault from inception to maturity within Vinculum Protocol. First-time wallets are ineligible for priority queue access regardless of deposit size. Loyalty is a prerequisite for speed.
Rule 4A.3
Priority Queue Fee

Eligible wallets may pay a priority fee to advance their position. The fee is dynamic based on current queue depth:

Priority Fee = (Weeks of Queue Backlog) × 0.5% of Deposit Value

Hard cap: 5.0% of deposit value regardless of queue length.

Rule 4A.4
Priority Fee Distribution

Priority fees collected are split as follows:

  • 50% → Core Reserve Pool
  • 50% → Ecosystem Fund (distributed to VCLM holders)

Priority fees are never used for new VCLM minting and never flow to the Dev Fund.

Rule 4A.5
Priority Does Not Affect Mint Amount
Paying a priority fee advances queue position only. It does not increase, decrease, or otherwise affect the VCLM minted. The mint formula defined in Rule 2.2 applies identically to all positions regardless of priority status.
Rule 4A.6
One Priority Upgrade Per Deposit
A wallet may pay the priority fee once per vault deposit. Priority cannot be purchased multiple times for the same deposit. Among priority queue participants, order is still first-in-first-out.
Section 05
Rewards for VCLM Holders
Rule 5.1
Revenue Share
VCLM holders are eligible to receive a proportional share of protocol revenue, including transaction fees, treasury yield, and partnership distributions.
Rule 5.2
Revenue Share Paid in External Assets
Revenue share distributions are paid in ETH, USDC, or other approved stablecoins — never in newly minted VCLM. This prevents reward compounding from inflating supply.
Rule 5.3
No Yield from Minting
VCLM minting is a one-time event triggered by a vault deposit. It is not an ongoing yield mechanism. Yield paid to collateral stakers comes exclusively from protocol revenue.
Section 06
Protocol 2 and Future VCLM Staking
Immutable
Rule 6.1
VCLM Staking Is Impossible in Protocol 1
VCLM staking is technically impossible within Protocol 1. It is not a policy restriction — it is a hard contract-level enforcement. No governance action can enable VCLM staking within Protocol 1.
Rule 6.2
Protocol 2 Activation Conditions

A second protocol in which VCLM may be staked may only be activated when all of the following conditions are simultaneously true:

  1. VCLM is actively listed and trading on at least one recognized external exchange
  2. VCLM circulating market cap has exceeded $100,000,000 (one hundred million USD) and sustained that level for a minimum of 30 continuous days
  3. Protocol 1 has been live for a minimum of 12 continuous months with no critical security incidents
  4. A DAO governance supermajority vote of >66% approval passes with the required quorum
  5. An independent third-party security audit of Protocol 2 has been completed and published
Rule 6.2a
Protocol 2 Vote Time-Lock
Once all objective conditions in Rule 6.2 items 1 through 3 and 5 are simultaneously met and verified on-chain, the governance vote required by item 4 must open within 14 days and must remain open for the standard 14-day voting window. No governance bloc, wallet, or combination of wallets may delay, postpone, or prevent the vote from opening. This rule exists to prevent any actor from using governance position as a timing weapon to delay a price-moving event for personal trading advantage.
Rule 6.3
Protocol 2 Staking Rewards
VCLM staked in Protocol 2 earns protocol revenue share and governance weight only. It does not trigger new VCLM mints. The anti-inflation rules of Protocol 1 remain intact within Protocol 2.
Section 07
Enforcement
Rule 7.1
Smart Contract Enforcement
Rules 1.2, 1.3, 2.1, 4.1, 4.2, 4.3, and 6.1 shall be enforced directly in the protocol's smart contracts. The contracts make violations technically impossible, not merely prohibited by policy.
Rule 7.2
Audit Requirement
Prior to mainnet launch, all staking and minting contracts must be audited by at least one independent third-party security firm. The audit report shall be published publicly.
Immutable
Rule 7.3
Immutable Rules

The following rules are immutable and cannot be changed by any governance vote under any circumstance:

  • Rule 1.2 — VCLM is not stakeable in Protocol 1
  • Rule 4.1 — Hard maximum supply cap of 10 billion
Rule 7.4
Governance Amendment Process

All rules not listed in Rule 7.3 may be amended by a DAO governance vote requiring:

  • Greater than 50% quorum of eligible voting power
  • Greater than 60% approval of votes cast
  • A 14-day public voting window
Rule 7.5
Epoch Cap Voting Conflict of Interest

Proposals to adjust the epoch mint cap represent a direct financial conflict of interest for large VCLM holders. To mitigate this conflict the following restrictions apply to epoch cap votes specifically:

  • Any wallet holding more than 1% of total circulating VCLM supply has its voting weight on epoch cap proposals capped at 1% of total supply regardless of actual holdings
  • This cap cannot be circumvented by distributing holdings across multiple wallets
  • All other governance proposals are unaffected by this rule
  • This rule is itself amendable only by a governance vote in which the 1% cap applies
Section 08
Transparency Commitments
Vinculum Protocol deliberately does not attempt to engineer out all forms of market turbulence, speculation, or organic price behavior. Every rule added to a protocol introduces new complexity, new edge cases, and new interactions that cannot be fully anticipated. Overcompensating for theoretical risks often creates more complicated and less understood risks in their place. The protocol therefore draws a clear distinction between behavior that is genuinely extractive or manipulative — which warrants a rule — and behavior that is simply markets operating as markets — which warrants transparency, not prohibition.
Rule 8.1
Vault Unlock Calendar
The protocol shall publish and maintain a publicly accessible vault unlock calendar showing the approximate timeline of all major VCLM vesting events derivable from on-chain data. This calendar shall be updated continuously and shall be accessible without requiring a wallet connection or account.
Rule 8.2
Collateral Backing Ratio Dashboard
The Collateral Backing Ratio (CBR) shall be displayed publicly and in real time on the protocol's primary interface. Participants shall be able to see the current CBR, its 30-day trend, and the 0.50 threshold at which minting pauses. No participant should ever be surprised by a mint pause.
Rule 8.3
Volatile Asset Risk Disclosure
At the point of deposit for any Tier B or Tier C collateral asset, the protocol interface shall display a prominent disclosure stating that the asset is classified as volatile, that its USD value may decline significantly during the lock period, that the staker will receive the original nominal token amount at maturity regardless of price change, and that the 5% retention fee is non-refundable. The staker must affirmatively acknowledge this disclosure before the deposit transaction is submitted.
Rule 8.4
Per-Asset Deposit Cap Transparency
The current per-asset deposit cap for every approved collateral asset shall be publicly visible alongside the current total deposited for that asset. Participants shall be able to see how much remaining capacity exists for any given asset before committing a deposit.
Rule 8.5
Priority Queue Depth Visibility
The current queue depth, estimated wait time in epochs, and current priority fee rate shall be displayed publicly and in real time on the protocol interface. Participants shall have full information about queue conditions before deciding whether to pay a priority fee or wait.
