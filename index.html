The Mechanism

Simple by design.

Three steps. No hidden mechanics. No yield farming risk. Your tokens do exactly one thing while they wait — nothing.

STEP 01
🔒
Lock your tokens

Choose an approved asset, an amount, and a duration — 7 days to 730 days. Approve the contract. Commit. Your tokens transfer to the VaultManager contract on Base and stay there. No lending. No yield farming. No exposure to any other protocol's risk. They just wait.

→
STEP 02
⬡
Earn $VCLM instantly

The moment you commit, VCLM is minted to your wallet. The amount is calculated based on the dollar value of your deposit, the asset quality tier, and your chosen duration. Longer commitment = more VCLM. There is no other way to acquire VCLM.

→
STEP 03
✓
Withdraw at maturity

When your vault matures, withdraw your principal. The protocol permanently retains 5% — split across three treasury pools governed by VCLM holders. Your VCLM was already in your wallet from day one. You don't wait for a reward. You already have it.

The Token

$VCLM

Every VCLM in existence was minted against real locked value. There is no VCLM that wasn't earned. No founder allocation. No investor round. No airdrop. The entire supply — every single token — represents someone who committed real assets and waited.

The governance token is backed by the behavior that created it. VCLM is not a receipt. Not a reward token that dilutes to zero. It is proof of behavior — the on-chain record that you committed and you waited. You don't buy it. You earn it.

🗳️
Governance Rights

Vote on protocol parameters, new vault assets, treasury deployment, and ecosystem direction. Validator-staked VCLM carries 1.5× voting weight. The protocol evolves through its committed holders — nobody else.

📉
Emission Decay

Emission decreases 20% per year, floored at 10% of the original rate. At launch: 10 VCLM per $1.00 committed. Year 5: ~3.3 VCLM per $1.00. Early commitment earns proportionally more — permanently.

🌐
Interchain Native

Built on Axelar Interchain Token Service from day one. One canonical VCLM supply across every chain — not fragmented bridged tokens. VCLM earned on any chain is the same token, with the same governance weight.

⬡
Standard ERC-20

VCLM is a standard ERC-20 token. Once earned, it belongs to you — hold it for governance, provide liquidity, trade it. The protocol never sells VCLM and has no mechanism to take it back.

Questions

Straight answers.

Your tokens transfer to the VaultManager smart contract on Base. They stay there until your vault matures. Nothing else happens to them. They are not lent out. They are not used for yield farming. They are not exposed to any secondary protocol's risk. The contract holds them and returns them at maturity — minus the 5% protocol retention. The simplicity is intentional. This protocol was designed for holders who have been burned by complexity before.
The protocol never sells VCLM. There was no presale. There is no premine. No founder allocation. No investor round. No airdrop. The only acquisition method through Vinculum is committing tokens to a vault. However — VCLM is a standard ERC-20 token. Once someone earns it, it belongs to them and they can do whatever they want with it — including providing liquidity on a DEX. Once liquidity pools exist, VCLM will be tradeable on the open market. The "can't buy it" statement refers to the protocol itself, not the secondary market that will emerge organically from holders.
Right now — no market price exists. At launch, the market will decide. But consider this: VCLM is the sole governance token for a protocol with real TVL locked in it. Every VCLM in existence was minted against real committed value. The emission rate decays 20% per year — early minters earn 10 VCLM per $1 committed. Year 5 minters earn roughly 3.3. What is governance rights over a growing, self-sustaining protocol worth to someone who can never buy a founder allocation? That is the question the market will answer.
The 5% retention is what builds the protocol treasury — the permanent financial foundation that makes Vinculum self-sustaining. It funds security audits, infrastructure, and eventually the ecosystem growth that benefits all VCLM holders. It is not a fee paid to a founder. It goes into three treasury pools governed by VCLM holders. You are not paying 5% for a service. You are contributing 5% to a protocol you now govern.
Your vault sits in a matured state until you choose to withdraw. Nothing is automatic. When you're ready, you call the withdraw function in the dApp and your principal is returned to your wallet. Your VCLM was already in your wallet from the moment you committed — you don't wait for it at maturity.
